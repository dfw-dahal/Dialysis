What is Dialysis?

Dialysis is a life-saving treatment for people with kidney failure. It performs the essential functions of the kidneys when they can no longer do so effectively. There are two main types of dialysis:

  • Hemodialysis: Blood is pumped out of your body to an artificial kidney machine and returned to your body by tubes that connect you to the machine.
  • Peritoneal Dialysis: The inside lining of your belly acts as a natural filter. Wastes are taken out by means of a cleansing fluid called dialysate, which is washed in and out of your belly in cycles.

Dialysis helps to:

  • Remove waste, salt, and extra water to prevent them from building up in the body
  • Keep a safe level of certain chemicals in your blood, such as potassium, sodium, and bicarbonate
  • Help control blood pressure

Living with dialysis requires careful management of diet, fluid intake, and overall health. The following sections provide tips to help you maintain your health while on dialysis.

Health Tips for Dialysis Patients

Diet

  • Limit fluid intake
  • Control potassium and phosphorus intake
  • Monitor protein consumption
  • Reduce sodium intake

Exercise

  • Engage in low-impact activities
  • Try walking, swimming, or cycling
  • Aim for 30 minutes of activity most days
  • Consult your doctor before starting a new routine

Mental Well-being

  • Practice stress-reduction techniques
  • Join support groups
  • Maintain social connections
  • Consider counseling if needed
